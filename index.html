Gymnastics

First World Championship

German Friedrich Ludwig Jahn invented the side bar, horizontal bar, parallel bars, balancing beam, and jumping events in the late 1700s. Gymnastics was a thriving sport in Germany. When Baron Pierre de Coubertin revived gymnastics, it was included in the programme of the first Olympic Games of our period in 1896. Canada competed at its first world-level FIG tournament in Melbourne in 1956 with just one male gymnast and one female gymnast . The first male gymast Curtis Hibbert earned a silver medal on the high bar at the 1987 World Championships in Rotterdam, it became Canada's first-ever medal at a World Championships.



History

The word gymnastics is taken from a Greek word. Before the Olympic games were abandoned in 393 CE, only tubling and vaulting were known in the ancient world. Many of these exercises eventually found their way into the Olympic games.



The Best Gymnast

Simone Biles is an American artistic gmnast. The most Olympic medals ever won by an American gymnast.  She currently holds the World Championship record for both the most gold medals (19) and the most medals overall (25). She also holds the record for most Olympic medals won by a female gymnast, with six total—four gold, one silver, and one bronze—won in world championships.
